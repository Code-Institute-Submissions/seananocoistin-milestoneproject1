## Faisnéis
Is suíomh gréasáin é www.tomhaltoir.com do dhaoine ar mian leo táirgí agus seirbhísí a fháil i nGaeilge. Tá eolas faoin gCumann le fáil ar an leathanach "Maidir Linn". Tig le daoine clárú le Cumann na dTomhaltóirí Gaeilge ar an suíomh gréasáin. Tá eolas ann freisin i mBéarla (**"Advice for Businesses"**) do ghnólachtaí ar mian leo comhairle a fháil faoi conas an Ghaeilge a bheith ina ngnó. 

## Úsáideoirí
Tá www.tomhaltoir.com dírithe ar dhá dhream faoi leith. Is iad daoine le Gaeilge an dream is mó air a bhfuil sé díríthe. Is éard atá ag teastáil ó na daoine sin ná eolas faoi tháirgí agus seirbhísí i nGaeilge. An dara dream air a bhfuil sé dírithe ná lucht gnó le Béarla atá sa tóir ar eolas agus comhairleoir maidir le conas feidhm a bhaint as an nGaeilge ina ngnólachtaí féin. Is dhá dhream éagsúil iad.
Do na tomhaltóirí Gaeilge, tá eolas ar an suíomh gréasáin i dtaobh an Chumainn (Maidir Linn) agus leathanach air ar féidir leo clárú leis an gCumann (Bí i do Bhall).

Don lucht gnó le Béarla, tá leathanach eolais ann i mBéarla chun comhairle, eolas, agus freagraí ar cheisteanna coitianta a thabhairt dóibh. Tig leo teagmháil a dhéanamh leis an gCumann trí bhithin Modal i gCeist 24 ag bun an leathanaigh information.html. Tá freisin nasc ag an mbun do leathanach Facebook.

Mar aon leis sin, rinne mé athruithe ar na comhaid HTML de réir a chéile. Mar shampla, chonaic mé go raibh an cnaipe Barr ag luí ar an roghchlár ag bun an leathanaigh. Bhí orm an bun a athrú ionas nach mbeidh an roghchlár i bhfolach. Nuair a d'athraigh mé é, bhí an roghchlár ar chlé agus an cnaipe ar dheis. Ní raibh an roghchlár i bhfolach a thuilleadh.

# Tomhaltoir.com
> A website for people who speak Irish who would like to buy products and services in the Irish langauge! Irish speakers who desire to buy products in the Irish language can get further information about Cumann na dTomhaltóirí Gaeilge and register as a member of the organisation. It contains information for businesses to help them to start offering products and services in the Irish language.

## Information
www.tomhaltoir.com is a website for Cumann na dTomhaltóirí Gaeilge, an assocation for people who wish to buy products and services in the Irish language. There is information about the Cumann on the page "Maidir Linn" (About us). People may register with the organisation on the website. There is information in English (**"Information and advice for Businesses"**) for businesses that are looking for advice about how to offer products and services in the Irish language. 

## Users
www.tomhaltoir.com has two distinct target audiences.

These are, primarily, Irish-speaking consumers who are looking for products and services in the Irish language and, secondly, anglophone businesses that are looking for advice about how they can use the Irish language in their businesses.
For Irish-speaking consumers, the website is mainly in Irish for them and it provides information about the Cumann (Maidir Linn) as well as a member registration page (Bí i do Bhall). These Irish speakers need information and means of engaging in the Irish language.

For anglophone businesses, there is a large Information page in the English language offering much information, advice, best practices, and answers to commonly-asked questions. At the end of that page, there is a modal form in order for them to make contact with the Cumann. For further interaction and information, there is a link to a Facebook page in the footer.

## Testing
I tested the HTML pages on https://validator.w3.org/, the CSS stylesheet on https://jigsaw.w3.org/css-validator/ and the JavaScript file on https://www.jslint.com/ .
I did that at least three times. It helped to find errors in the files and to correct them.

Along with that, I made changes to the HTML files as the project was progressing. For example, I saw that the Barr button was lying over the footer menu. I had to change it so that it would no longer obscure the menu. When I changed it, the menu was to the left of the button and the button was to the right who no part of the menu under it anymore.

I tested the website on Chrome, Microsoft Edge, Firefox, and Adblock Browser on a mobile telephone to see was the website functioning as it ought to. I saw in Chrome on the telephone that it was not right, so I had to change some things in order for headings and text widths be right and prevent horizontal scrolling.
An example of that can be seen in this image that was taken from Chrome on a Samsung handset:
http://www.tomhaltoir.com/assets/images/testingscreenshot.jpg

I put the CSS file into https://autoprefixer.github.io/ to make it suitable for different browsers.
